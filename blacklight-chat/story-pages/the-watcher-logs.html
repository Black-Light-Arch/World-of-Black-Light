THE WATCHER LOGS

Archive Entry — Recovered Field Records

The following entries were recovered from a waterproof recorder found at the edge of the restricted zone. No body was located. The case remains open.

—

LOG ENTRY 01 — 03:12

I have been watching the building for six hours. Nothing moves. Nothing should be able to move — every entrance is sealed, every window boarded. And yet I keep catching something in my peripheral vision. A shift. A shape. Gone before I can focus.

I am starting to question whether the movement is outside the building at all.

—

LOG ENTRY 02 — 04:47

I went inside.

I know the protocol. I know what I was told. But the readings spiked and I had to follow.

The ground floor is clean. Dust undisturbed except for one thing: a perfect circle cleared in the center of the main hall, as if something stood there recently. Stood very still. For a very long time.

The air in the circle is several degrees colder than the rest of the room.

I did not step into it.

—

LOG ENTRY 03 — 05:30

I found the photographs on the second floor.

Dozens of them. Nailed to every wall in the eastern corridor, overlapping, covering every inch of surface. Each one shows the same subject — a different person, at night, outside a building.

They do not know they are being photographed.

The most recent photograph on the wall is dated three days ago.

It shows a figure standing at the edge of a tree line, holding a recorder, staring at a dark building.

It takes me several seconds to understand what I am looking at.

—

LOG ENTRY 04 — 05:51

I am not going to leave.

I do not mean that as a statement of intention. I mean it as a fact I have come to accept. Every route I have tried has returned me here — to this corridor, to these photographs. The building rearranges itself. Or I do.

There is a door at the end of the hall that was not here before.

It is open.

There is light inside. Soft. Red. Pulsing.

—

LOG ENTRY 05 — 06:17

I am inside the room now.

There is a chair. There is a figure in the chair. It has not moved since I entered and I do not believe it will.

On the wall above it, pinned beside a dozen others, is a blank white card. Waiting.

I understand now what the circle of cleared dust was for.

I understand now why the building let me in.

I understand now what kind of watcher I have always been.

—

The recorder was found with the battery depleted. The final entry was not logged by voice. It was logged by the machine continuing to run in an empty room — recording only silence, and the slow rhythm of something breathing that was not human.

The blank card on the wall was no longer blank.

Archive closed. File 002 sealed.
